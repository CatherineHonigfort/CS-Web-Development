Have you seen the newest musical "Hamilton"?

Hamilton, the newest broadway musical, is sweeping the nation, and with good reason.

"Hamilton" tells the life and times of America's founding father who is featured on the $10 bill. Notable achievements of Alexander Hamilton include:

  • Founder of the National Treasury
  • Founder of the New York Times Newspaper
  • Decorated soldier who was instrumental in the Revolutionary War
  • Active Member of the original Congress and Cabinet
  • Author of 51 of the Federalist Papers

This musical starts in the British West Indies where Alexander Hamilton was born, and follows his life as he travels, against all odds, to New York to attend King's College. In New York is where he meets other men and women who will be remembered in the history books, such as Aaron Burr, French fighter Marie-Joseph Paul Lafayette, his future wife Eliza Schuyler, General George Washington, and many others. Through the multitude of well crafted songs, the audience learns about the revolutionary war, George Washington's presidency, John Adam's presidency, and finally up to the first weeks of Thomas Jefferson's presidency.

You may be asking yourself, "Who would even think to use music to describe America's least remembered founding father?"

Glad you asked! His name is Lin Manuel miranda, and he is also the man who plays Alexander Hamilton on Broadway.

He spent 6 years drafting this musical. After an excessive amount of research, his play has hit the big stage, and he has won three Tony awards for his efforts: Best Book of a Musical, Best Musical, and Best Original Score. Over all, Hamilton has been nominated for 16 different Tonys and has won 11 of them.
